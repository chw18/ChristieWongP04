BlackBerry 10 Longitudinal Study

focuses on how people learn a BlackBerry Z10 or Q10 device

User-Oriented App Project

Role
Usability Researcher

Client
BlackBerry

Year / Time Taken
2012 - 2013 / 8 months

Tools
User experience research methods, Ovo Studios software

Purpose
The goal of this study was to conduct research and gain insights on how users learn and discover the features of BlackBerry 10 over a period of one week.

My Contribution

  • Spent 100+ hr in lab (including preparation time)
  • Interacted with 30 participants
  • Ran this study for 9 cycles
  • Produced 7 reports
  • Findings were communicated to CSO, Design, Marketing, Product Management, Product Information Design and executives
  • Increased awareness of the importance of longitudinal data

Result
Through our research, we gained a better understanding on the learnability of BlackBerry 10 and conveyed these findings back to different departments.

A Typical Cycle

Preparation Work

  • Protocol of study
  • Printed materials including consent forms, questionnaires and diaries
  • Coding system to determine participant‘s learning process
  • Log to record data

Interaction with Participants

  • Recruit participants, only accepting those with no previous working knowledge of the BlackBerry 10 device
  • Facilitate one-on-one sessions, 3x a week with each participant

Hardware & Software Knowledge

  • Constantly learn and be familiarized with changes in BlackBerry 10 OS
  • Continue my hardware understanding of the BlackBerry 10 devices
  • Upload different builds on devices for each cycle
  • Learn technology in the labs to video record sessions

Research Findings

  • Analyze data and discuss findings with other researchers
  • Organize findings in a compelling written report, usually in a PowerPoint format
  • Present findings to rest of team and other departments
