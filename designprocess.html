My Design Process

The way I approach problems varies and depends on the specific design problem itself. However, my overall process consists of the same essential components that are shown below.

Though the components are shown in a linear fashion, my design process is not strictly in this order and there are times when I wander back and forth at certain phases. The diagram below is just a general sense of how my design process works.

Receiving the Design Brief

Being thoroughly informed on the design problem and the client‘s needs, asking questions for clarification if necessary

Brainstorming Initial Ideas

Generate rough ideas that could develop into possible solutions

Sketching out Ideas

Using paper and pencil, I sketch the ideas I believe have potential

Conducting Background Research

If I am not familiar with topics involved in this design problem, I look online for informative resources in that area

Getting Inspired

Design blogs are my main sources of inspiration; otherwise, I get inspired and combine concepts from different areas (eg. music, paintings, etc.). This phase tends to overlap with the research phase

Acquiring Insights from the User

There‘s nothing better than just asking for feedback from the intended users, which is done in person or online

Running into "Creative" Blocks

Similar to a "writer‘s block," there are times when I can‘t seem to go any further in my ideation; when this happens, I go back to the inspiration phase

Prototyping ideas

Depending on the type of ideas generated, prototypes can be in the form of sketches, paper models, interactive mockups, 3D structures and more. Quite a bit of time is spent in this phase as I need to continuously re-evaluate and try different ideas

Delivering Final Idea

Given the time span requested by the client, I present my best design solution. The format for this would depend on the nature of the solution itself and the client‘s preferences.
